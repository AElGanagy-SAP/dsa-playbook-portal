✓ What to Do
  • Dig into the real business problems — don't just take the brief at face value.
  • Make the customer's pain feel real and show how SAP solves it.
  • Paint a picture of what great looks like — use design thinking, demo days, or industry stories.
  • Get your AEs and partners on the same page early — no surprises later.
✓ What to Do
  • Run demos that speak to their world — tackle real pain points, not just features.
  • Sort out architecture, integration, and security early so it doesn't come back to bite you.
  • Build a business case that hits home — lean on DVA support to back it up.
  • Pull in the right people at the right time — don't let doubts linger.
✓ What to Do
  • Hand off the full picture — goals, KPIs, and what success looks like (to be aligned).
  • Tell the value story to post-sales so they can grow and expand the account.
✗ What to Avoid
  • Don't jump into a demo before you understand what they actually care about.
  • Don't go deep on tech before everyone agrees on the direction.
  • Don't go it alone — align with your account team first.
  • Don't commit to scope before the sponsor is fully on board.
✗ What to Avoid
  • Don't run a generic demo — if it's not about them, they'll tune out.
  • Don't get lost in tech talk without tying it back to their business.
  • Don't wait until it's urgent to bring in specialists.
  • Don't lose track of what they actually need to say yes.
✗ What to Avoid
  • Don't get pulled into delivery — that's not your lane.
  • Don't step in for Customer Success or Adoption — let them do their job.
  • Don't spend energy where there's no deal or expansion on the table.
