DSA Pipeline Progression

Real-world situations to guide DSA decisions across the pipeline lifecycle (FY26)

Pipeline Progression North Star

Pipeline Progression improves when more pipeline converts to Booked/Won, weak deals are removed early, and new pipeline has a real shot to close.

→ If it doesn't move a deal forward or remove it from pipeline — it's not helping your number.
🔍 Explore Phase
Scenario 01Explore
✗ DSA Declines
AE
Account Executive

We met a prospect at a conference — they want a demo next week but don't want to sit through another discovery call. Can you just run it?

DSA
✗ DSA — Not without context

A demo without context becomes a generic product walkthrough — the exact thing this customer is already tired of seeing. Without knowing what actually matters to them, there's no way to make the session worth their time.

DSA
DSA — Here's a middle ground

Let me frame a short 20-minute pre-call as a "pre-demo briefing" — positioned as something that makes the demo sharper and more relevant to their world. That's usually all I need, and customers rarely push back when it's framed that way.

💡 Why this aligns with the playbook

Jumping into demos without understanding business context is exactly what the Explore phase warns against. The DSA's role is to improve pipeline quality at the source — not add motion without direction.

Scenario 02Explore
✓ DSA Accepts
AE
Account Executive

I've got a CFO who's frustrated with slow financial close cycles — they have a board presentation in 6 weeks and are actively looking for a solution. Can you join the next call to help structure the discovery and sharpen the use case?

DSA
✓ DSA — This is the right call to invest in

Real pain, senior decision-maker, and a hard deadline — this is exactly the opportunity worth jumping on at Explore stage. I'll come prepared to validate the pain, confirm the decision path, and lock in a use case that can anchor the Select conversation.

💡 Why this aligns with the playbook

A real problem, a real buyer, and a time-bound trigger are all present. Structured discovery here forces the clarity — use case, buying trigger, decision path — that the playbook requires before moving forward.

🎯 Select Phase
Scenario 03Select
✓ DSA Accepts
AE
Account Executive

We had a demo two months ago that generated interest but no commitment. I've managed to get another meeting with the VP of Operations — can you come in with something sharper this time?

DSA
✓ DSA — I'm in, but on my terms

I'm not repeating the first session. Share everything you know about the VP's specific frustrations — I'll build a scenario that mirrors their reality and is designed to end with a decision, not just interest.

DSA
DSA — What this session will look like

We go in with one clear goal: break the stall, not extend it. The session ends with a yes, a no, or a rescoped conversation — all three are better outcomes than walking away with "they're still interested."

💡 Why this aligns with the playbook

This is the Select phase in action — attacking a stalled opportunity with a targeted approach designed to unlock a decision. The DSA sets the right expectation: every demo must drive an outcome, not just generate interest.

Scenario 04Select
✗ DSA Declines
AE
Account Executive

A deal has been in pipeline for 5 months but the original champion left and we haven't found a replacement. The ACV is low but I think a thorough technical deep-dive might re-energise the opportunity. Can you help?

DSA
✗ DSA — No active buyer, no live session

Days of prep for a deal with no champion isn't a responsible use of my time — without a buyer in the room, even a great session goes nowhere.

DSA
DSA — Here's what I can do instead

I'll pull together a pre-built industry demo recording, a relevant customer success story, and a concise value snapshot for their sector. If someone engages with those assets and a new stakeholder steps forward, that becomes my trigger to re-engage properly — and the deal earns its way back into active pipeline.

💡 Why this aligns with the playbook

The playbook is clear — don't spend time on deals with no buyer and don't over-engineer low-probability opportunities. Reusable assets let the AE keep the door open without pulling the DSA away from deals that can actually move this quarter.

📦 Derive Phase
Scenario 05Derive
✗ DSA Declines
AE
Account Executive

A deal we closed three months ago is now in implementation. Would you be able to join the upcoming quarterly business review? It'd really help strengthen the relationship.

DSA
✗ DSA — Not without an expansion trigger

At this stage the relationship belongs to Customer Success — stepping in without a specific reason adds no value and pulls time away from active pipeline.

DSA
DSA — When I would engage

If a new business pain or expansion opportunity has surfaced — a new department, a process they want to improve — I'm happy to join with a clear purpose. Without that trigger, the QBR is a Customer Success conversation, not a DSA one.

💡 Why this aligns with the playbook

The Derive phase explicitly says to avoid attending QBRs without a clear expansion intent and to not replace the Customer Success role. DSA involvement post-sale without a business reason costs pipeline time elsewhere and doesn't move the KPI.

💡 Innovate Phase
Scenario 06Innovate
✗ DSA Declines
AE
Account Executive

I'd love to run an open-ended AI innovation workshop with one of our existing customers — nothing specific in mind yet, just exploring what AI could do for them and deepening the relationship.

DSA
✗ DSA — No real trigger, no workshop

Without a business trigger, this becomes an interesting conversation that leads nowhere — the customer walks away impressed but without a problem to solve, and I walk away with no qualified pipeline.

DSA
DSA — Here's how to make it work

Let's work with you and the CSM to first identify a specific operational challenge or growth goal that AI could actually address. Once there's a real trigger, I'll design the workshop around it — with a clear purpose and a realistic path to a qualified opportunity.

💡 Why this aligns with the playbook

The Innovate phase says to run expansion discovery only when there's a real business trigger. Innovation without a buying trigger creates activity, not pipeline. The DSA reframes rather than simply refusing — protecting pipeline quality while keeping the relationship intact.
